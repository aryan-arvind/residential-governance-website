About Our Society

Home to 120 families.
A community since 1998.

Rishikesh Co-operative Housing Society is a thriving residential community in Sector-34, Kamothe, Mansarovar, Navi Mumbai. Four wings, 120 flats, and a legacy of transparent, resident-first management.

Our society is registered under the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act 1960 and governed by a democratically elected Managing Committee. From AMC contractors to water supply, parking to security — every decision is made collectively and transparently.

FAQ

Common questions from our residents.

Still need help? Email the office or contact the secretary.

Log in to the member portal using your flat number and registered mobile number. Go to Pay Maintenance, select the billing period, and pay using UPI, net banking, or debit/credit card. Your receipt is automatically generated and emailed to you.

Log in to the member portal, go to Document Requests → NOC Request, select the type (passport, bank loan, meter transfer), and submit. The secretary will review and approve your request. The formatted NOC letter will be available for download in your portal within 2 working days.

Go to Raise a Complaint in your member portal. Select the category (Plumbing, Electrical, Lift, Security, Housekeeping, etc.), describe the issue, and submit. You'll receive a complaint number and can track the status in real time. Urgent issues can also be reported directly to the secretary.

Yes — society bye-laws require tenant registration. Log in as an owner, go to Tenant Management, and add your tenant's details (name, ID proof, contact, police verification status, lease start and end date). The committee will be notified and the tenant information will be recorded in the society register.

The AGM for the current year is scheduled on 5th May 2025 at 7:00 PM in the Society Hall. The official notice and agenda have been circulated. Log in to the member portal and go to Notices → AGM 2025 to view the full agenda. All members are requested to attend.

A late fee of ₹50 per month is applicable after the due date as per society bye-laws. After 3 months of default, the committee is authorized to issue a formal reminder. Continued default leads to a Section 101 Notice under MCS Act 1960. Please clear dues on time to avoid action.
